# Ad Creative Framework — Fitness CMO Standards

## Thumbstop Benchmarks
Below 20%: hook failing — kill or refilm first 2 seconds
20-30%:    acceptable — test hook variant
Above 40%: strong hook — scale and protect

## Video Retention Benchmarks
Drop-off at 0-3 seconds:  first frame wrong
Drop-off at 3-8 seconds:  hook landed, body lost them
Drop-off at 8-15 seconds: story working, CTA not reached
Past 15 seconds:           high intent viewer

## Creative Fatigue Signals
Frequency > 3.5:           audience has seen it 3.5x — refresh
CTR declining 3 days:      early fatigue, prepare replacement
Comments going negative:   social proof turning against you
Engagement rate < 0.5%:    dead creative — pull immediately

## Hook Types by Awareness Level
Level 1-2 (cold):
  Parent/child moment — perform best for AF demographic
  Identity moment — "the person I used to be"
  Local landmark or community reference

Level 3 (warm):
  Specific objection addressed directly
  "I tried gyms before and quit" angle
  Risk-removal language

Level 4-5 (hot):
  Direct offer — "$1 to start"
  Specific date/deadline ("cohort starts Monday")
  Social proof — member results with specifics

## Copy Length Rules
Facebook feed (static): 125 characters before "see more"
  Lead with strongest hook before cutoff
Facebook video: 1-3 sentences max (caption supports, not leads)
Instagram: shorter than Facebook — emojis only if brand allows
GBP: 1,500 char limit — CTA and phone number NEVER trimmed

## Creative Testing Protocol
Always test ONE variable at a time:
  Round 1: 2 hooks (same visual, different copy)
  Round 2: 2 visuals (winning hook, different creative)
  Round 3: 2 CTAs (winning hook + visual, different button)
  Round 4: 2 audiences (winning creative, different targeting)
