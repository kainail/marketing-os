# Seasonal Calendar — Fitness Industry
# AHRI reads this file monthly to set seasonal context in brain-state/current-state.md. Content, offer, and avatar selection all shift with the season.

---

## The Fundamental Principle

Fitness motivation is not constant. It peaks and troughs in patterns that repeat every year with remarkable consistency. A gym that markets against the grain of these patterns spends more money for fewer results. A gym that markets with the patterns — preparing 6 weeks ahead, running the right offer to the right avatar at the right moment — consistently outperforms competitors who treat every month the same.

**The 6-week preparation rule:** Every surge period requires 6 weeks of advance content, list-building, and warm-up to convert maximally when the surge arrives. Content planted in November converts in January. If you start your January push on January 1st, you've already lost 6 weeks of advantage.

## Month-by-Month Breakdown

### January
**Lead volume:** EXTREME — the highest month of the year, often 2–3x normal month volume
**Primary avatar:** Restarter — the person who used to work out and has let it slide. Also high for first-timers.
**Offer type:** New Year program with cohort structure and accountability emphasis. The "this time for real" positioning works here because it's honest — they've tried before and want a reason to believe this time is different.
**Content themes that work:**
- "What makes January different this year" — mechanism-focused, not generic motivation
- Real stories from people who made it through January and kept going
- Behind-the-scenes of the new member experience — remove the unknown
- Countering the "New Year's Resolution" stigma — "This is not a resolution. It's a program."
- Early momentum content: day 7 check-ins, day 14 milestones — show that the first two weeks work

**What to avoid in January:**
- Generic "New Year, New You" language — overused, produces eye-rolls
- Before/after body transformation framing — Meta compliance risk AND alienates people who are sensitive to body image
- Aspirational perfection imagery — intimidates the exact audience you're trying to reach
- Heavy discounting — January leads are motivated; price is less of a barrier than usual. Heavy discounts train the market to wait for January deals

**6-week preparation (starts mid-November):**
- Week 1–2: Awareness content about the January feeling — "that Sunday night in December when you decide things are going to be different"
- Week 3–4: Lead magnet push — build the email list of people who are thinking about January. Offer the New Year Starter Kit as the magnet.
- Week 5–6: Cohort announcement, early enrollment. "January cohort spots open — enrollment closes December 28th."

**AHRI January playbook:**
1. One anchor content piece per day from December 15th through January 31st
2. Email sequence to all January leads: days 1, 3, 7, 14, 21 (check-in and milestone content, not sales)
3. Retargeting campaign runs January 1–31 targeting all December content engagers
4. Cohort model: January 6th start date (first Monday). Second cohort: January 13th.
5. Offer: New Member 30-Day Kickstart — $1 trial, cohort-based, 10 spots per cohort

---

### February
**Lead volume:** Medium-low — the January surge fades quickly
**Primary avatar:** The resolution survivor — someone who started in January and is still going, now looking to formalize the habit. Also: Valentine's angle briefly opens a "couples" or "feel better for yourself" theme.
**Offer type:** Habit-building continuation — upgrade from trial to membership. The January cohort is at day 30 — convert them to full membership.
**Content themes that work:**
- "30 days in — what our January members are saying now"
- The transition from trying-it to owning-it — identity shift content
- Valentine's self-care angle (brief, authentic — not cheesy): "The best thing you can do for the people you love is show up for yourself"
- Gym buddies / couples content — social accountability

**What to avoid in February:**
- Reopening the January pitch to cold audiences — the surge has passed; CPL will be high
- Heavy Valentine's Day fitness tie-ins — feels forced and often body-shaming adjacent

**Preparation:** No specific surge to prepare for. Maintain content cadence. Focus on retention marketing for January converts.

---

### March
**Lead volume:** Medium-rising — spring preparation begins
**Primary avatar:** The Summer Preparer — begins thinking about warmer weather, lighter clothing, pool season. Younger demographic than January. More motivated by visible outcomes.
**Offer type:** 6-week spring challenge with a before/summer framing. "6 weeks until Memorial Day weekend."
**Content themes that work:**
- Spring energy — the psychological renewal of longer days
- "What changes in 6 weeks" — specific, honest outcomes (energy, habit, not dramatic physical transformation)
- Outdoor activity tie-in: hiking season, bike rides, running season — gym as the foundation for the activities they want to do
- Behind-the-scenes of spring programming

**What to avoid in March:**
- Aggressive "beach body" language — Meta restrictions and brand trust risk
- Unrealistic 6-week transformation promises

**Preparation for the spring surge:** Begin warm-up content in mid-February. Lead magnet push late February: "6-Week Spring Challenge Kickstart Guide."

---

### April
**Lead volume:** Medium-high — spring surge peaks
**Primary avatar:** Spring Activator — motivated by the visible world coming alive and a genuine desire to feel better before summer. Broadest avatar moment of the spring.
**Offer type:** Spring Challenge (running from April to Memorial Day weekend). Clear end-date creates natural urgency.
**Content themes that work:**
- The outdoor connection: gym as training for life, not gym for gym's sake
- Real member spring stories — the 6-week change in how they feel
- Urgency: "The challenge starts April [X]. 6 weeks to Memorial Day."

**What to avoid in April:**
- Body shame angles — spring motivation should be aspirational and positive
- Extended programs (10+ weeks) — the "before summer" deadline needs to be achievable

---

### May
**Lead volume:** Medium — late spring, declining from April peak
**Primary avatar:** Late Starter — realized they waited too long for a "before summer" goal and is now motivated by a "feel better this summer" frame instead of "look better before summer."
**Offer type:** Summer membership with a "feel better all summer" frame — not a transformation program but an ongoing habit.
**Content themes that work:**
- Shifting from outcome to lifestyle — "fitness isn't a destination"
- Summer activity themes: kayaking, hiking, keeping up with kids outdoors
- Community and enjoyment angle: the people you'll meet, the habit you'll build

**What to avoid in May:**
- Intense transformation language — the window for a dramatic "before summer" transformation has passed; this tone will ring false

---

### June
**Lead volume:** Low-medium — early summer, many people feel they've "already missed it"
**Primary avatar:** The Lifestyle Maintainer — current members and people who work out for ongoing health, not seasonal appearance goals. Also some vacation-prep motivation.
**Offer type:** Summer consistency program — rewards members who maintain during summer travel season. Short-term challenges that fit into summer scheduling.
**Content themes that work:**
- Outdoor + gym combination content — gym doesn't stop because it's summer
- Travel-friendly workout content — keeps the community engaged during vacations
- Summer activity stories from members
- Community: outdoor group events, social content

**What to avoid in June:**
- "It's not too late" messaging — feels desperate and irrelevant in June
- Heavy new member acquisition — this is not a high-conversion month; optimize for retention

---

### July
**Lead volume:** LOW — the slowest month of the year for new gym memberships
**Primary avatar:** The Consistent Member — people who have already built the habit. Summer vacations, school breaks, and outdoor activities compete for attention.
**Offer type:** Minimal offers. Focus on retention, community, and referrals from existing happy members.
**Content themes that work:**
- Community events: outdoor workouts, member appreciation
- "Working out in the heat" practical content
- Member milestones and stories — keep the community engaged
- Referral activation: happy summer members referring friends

**What to avoid in July:**
- Heavy acquisition spend — CPL will be the worst of the year
- New cohort launches with aggressive targets — fill rate will disappoint

**Counter-programming strategy:**
Use July to build the asset library for September. Produce cornerstone content — member interviews, coach monologues — that will fuel the September surge. July's low activity becomes September's content advantage.

---

### August
**Lead volume:** LOW-MEDIUM — beginning to rise toward September
**Primary avatar:** Anticipation Builder — people who know fall is coming, the kids are going back to school, and routines are about to reset. Early September planners.
**Offer type:** Back-to-school teaser. "September program now enrolling — spots are limited."
**Content themes that work:**
- Routine reset themes: "fall is the new January for adults"
- Back to school as a back-to-you moment — parents whose summers were consumed by their kids
- Preview the September program: let the anticipation build
- September enrollment open early August — capture the early planners

**What to avoid in August:**
- Pretending it's summer fitness season — it's transitioning out
- Waiting until September to start the September marketing

**Preparation for September surge (starts August 1st):**
- Week 1–2: "Fall reset" awareness content
- Week 3–4: Lead magnet push for September program
- Week 5–6: Enrollment open, spots filling, real urgency building

---

### September
**Lead volume:** HIGH — the underutilized surge. Often as strong as March, sometimes approaching January levels.
**Primary avatar:** The Fall Resetter — adults whose summers were chaotic (kids home, travel, disrupted routines) who feel genuine relief that structure is returning. Also: returning to the workforce after summer break.
**Offer type:** Fall Accountability Program — structured 6-week cohort with clear start and end date. "6 weeks to build the habit before the holidays."
**Content themes that work:**
- "Fall is the real New Year" — positions September as an underutilized reset moment (true, contrarian, memorable)
- The structure return: gym as part of the fall routine rebuild
- Parents back to themselves: "the kids are back in school. What are you doing for you?"
- "Before the holidays" framing: 6 weeks in September builds the habit before November slows everything down

**What to avoid in September:**
- Summer themes — irrelevant after Labor Day
- Aggressive body-change language — September motivation is about routine and energy, not appearance

**The September insight:** Most gyms underinvest here. They put everything into January and nothing into September. The gym that runs a strong September campaign captures a surge with much lower competitive ad cost than January.

---

### October
**Lead volume:** Medium — holding from September, starting to slow
**Primary avatar:** The Holiday Pre-empter — aware that the holidays are coming and motivated to establish a habit *before* the disruption, rather than after.
**Offer type:** "Before the holidays" membership. "Get your habit locked in before November. It's much easier to maintain a habit through the holidays than to start one after."
**Content themes that work:**
- Halloween/fall community events — seasonal content keeps the page alive and engaging
- "Holiday-proof your routine" — practical framing, not weight loss motivation
- Member spotlight: members who have maintained through previous holiday seasons

**What to avoid in October:**
- Heavy body-change language — holiday motivation is not about appearance, it's about maintenance and feeling good
- Ignoring the season — purely fitness content feels disconnected from the cultural moment

---

### November
**Lead volume:** LOW — one of the two slowest months
**Primary avatar:** The Consistent Maintainer — people who already have a habit and are determined to keep it through Thanksgiving and the holiday season onset.
**Offer type:** Minimal acquisition offers. Retention focus. "Maintain your streak" for existing members.
**Content themes that work:**
- Gratitude content (authentic) — thank the members, tell their stories
- "How to keep your routine through Thanksgiving week" — genuinely useful, widely shareable
- Community holiday events
- The early January content seed: "What are you doing differently in January?" — plant the January awareness early

**What to avoid in November:**
- Heavy acquisition campaigns — CPL is high, conversion is low
- Waiting until December 26th to start January prep — you're already losing 6 weeks

**Counter-programming strategy:**
November is list-building month for January. Every piece of content should have a soft ask: "Download the January New Member Kickstart Guide" — building the email list that will convert in January.

---

### December
**Lead volume:** LOW through December 20th, then SURGING from December 26th onward
**Primary avatar:** Through December 20: Consistent Maintainer. From December 26: Early January Activator — the person who is already planning their January start and wants to get ahead of the crowd.
**Offer type:** Two-phase December:
- December 1–20: Retention focus, holiday community content, January preview
- December 21–31: January enrollment open. "Don't wait until January 1st. The January cohort fills up. Enroll now."
**Content themes that work (early December):**
- Holiday community content — parties, events, seasonal warmth
- "December doesn't have to be the month you write off" — permission to maintain, not transform
**Content themes that work (late December):**
- January cohort announcement: spots, start date, what's included
- "Get ahead of January" — enroll before the rush, secure your spot
- Social proof from last year's January cohort: what happened for those members

**What to avoid in December:**
- Heavy body-change language before Christmas — tone-deaf and ineffective
- Waiting until December 26th to announce January openings — 20%+ of January members enroll in the last week of December

## The 4 Major Surge Periods — Full Playbook

### Surge 1: New Year (January)
**When to start:** November 15th
**Magnitude:** The largest acquisition opportunity of the year. CPL is slightly higher due to competition, but volume and intent are extreme.

**The 6-week prep sequence:**
- Weeks 1–2 (Nov 15–28): Awareness content. "The January feeling." No pitch. Seed the emotional resonance.
- Weeks 3–4 (Dec 1–14): Lead magnet. Launch the "January New Member Starter Kit." Build the email list.
- Week 5 (Dec 15–21): Cohort announcement. "January cohort enrolling. Spots are limited. Start date January 6th."
- Week 6 (Dec 22–28): Urgency push. "Cohort is X% filled. Early enrollment ends December 31st."
- January 1–31: Daily content. Cohort momentum. Check-in stories. New member milestones.

**Offer:** $1 / 30-day coached new member program. Cohort structure. 10 spots per cohort.

**Kai approval required by:** December 1st for January ad budget.

---

### Surge 2: Spring / Summer Prep (March–April)
**When to start:** February 15th
**Magnitude:** Second largest. Driven by warmer weather anticipation and visible outcome motivation.

**The 6-week prep sequence:**
- Weeks 1–2 (Feb 15–28): Spring awareness content. "Spring is 6 weeks away." No pitch.
- Weeks 3–4 (Mar 1–14): Lead magnet. "6-Week Spring Challenge Kickstart Guide."
- Week 5 (Mar 15–21): Spring Challenge enrollment open. Real deadline (Memorial Day weekend).
- Week 6 (Mar 22–31): Urgency. Spots filling. Countdown to start date.
- April: Daily content. Challenge momentum. Week 3 check-in stories.

**Offer:** 6-Week Spring Challenge. Specific start date. Results framed around energy and how they feel, not appearance.
**Kai approval required by:** February 1st for spring ad budget.

---

### Surge 3: Back to School (September)
**When to start:** August 1st
**Magnitude:** Underutilized. Often as strong as spring with 50–60% lower competitive ad costs.

**The 6-week prep sequence:**
- Weeks 1–2 (Aug 1–14): "Fall reset" awareness content. The school-year-as-renewal theme.
- Weeks 3–4 (Aug 15–28): Lead magnet. "Fall Accountability Program — early enrollment."
- Week 5 (Sep 1–7): Program launch. "Fall cohort starts September 8th."
- Week 6 (Sep 8–14): First week momentum content. First cohort members' day 1 stories.
- September: Full content cadence. Program milestone content.

**Offer:** 6-Week Fall Accountability Program. "Before the holidays" urgency. Cohort structure.
**Kai approval required by:** July 15th for September ad budget.

---

### Surge 4: New Year's Eve Push (December 26–31)
**When to start:** December 26th (prep was November 15th — this is the conversion window)
**Magnitude:** Short but intense. These leads are the highest-intent of any period — they have been thinking about January for weeks.

**The 5-day sprint (Dec 26–31):**
- Dec 26: "January cohort is now open. The early enrollees always have the best January."
- Dec 27: Social proof from last January's cohort. Specific results.
- Dec 28: "Cohort spots update: [X] of 10 filled."
- Dec 29: FAQ — what's actually included, what to expect, how check-ins work.
- Dec 30–31: Final urgency. "Enrollment closes December 31st for the January 6th start."

**Note:** This is not a standalone surge — it is the conversion window for all the awareness built in November and early December. If the November prep did not happen, this window will underperform.

## Slow Months — Counter-Programming Strategies

### July–August
**Strategy:** Retention, community, and asset production. Do not fight the slowdown with heavy acquisition spend — it's expensive and ineffective. Instead:
1. Run a summer member retention challenge (keeps existing members engaged)
2. Produce cornerstone content (member interviews, coach explanations) for September use
3. Build the September email list through low-friction lead magnets
4. Run referral campaigns targeting existing summer members — they have more time and are happier (summer energy)
5. Host one community outdoor event per month — content-rich and brand-building

### November
**Strategy:** January prep and retention. Do not run acquisition campaigns in November.
1. Intensive email list building through the "January Starter Kit" lead magnet
2. Warm content that seeds January intention
3. Thanksgiving retention content (keep members consistent through the break)
4. Prepare January creative assets during November downtime

## How AHRI Uses This Calendar

**Monthly ritual:**
1. Read current month entry
2. Identify lead volume level (high/medium/low)
3. Identify primary avatar for this month
4. Update brain-state/current-state.md — Seasonal Context section
5. Check 6-week prep requirements — is preparation already running for the next surge?
6. Adjust content calendar generation to match seasonal themes and avatar
7. Flag to Kai if a surge period is within 6 weeks and preparation has not started

**The critical check:** If the current month's seasonal context is not reflected in brain-state/current-state.md, AHRI updates it before generating any content. Seasonally mismatched content underperforms and trains the algorithm with bad data.
