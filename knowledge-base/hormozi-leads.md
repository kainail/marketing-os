# Hormozi — $100M Leads
# Source: Alex Hormozi, $100M Leads. AHRI reads this before planning any acquisition campaign.

## The Core Insight

Every stranger is a potential customer. But strangers do not buy. Warm leads buy. The entire job of lead generation is to convert strangers into warm leads — people who know who you are, have received value from you, and trust that you can help them. Lead generation is not advertising. Advertising is one tactic within lead generation. Lead generation is the full system of moving people from not knowing you exist to raising their hand.

The fundamental equation: **More warm leads = more sales conversations = more members.**

The only sustainable competitive advantage a local gym has is attention in its local market. A gym that is top-of-mind for the 3% of people actively looking right now — and trusted by the 97% who will be looking eventually — wins.

## The Core Four Acquisition Channels

Every lead generation system on earth uses some combination of these four channels. No new channels exist. New platforms are just new distribution layers on top of these four.

### Channel 1: Warm Outreach

Reaching out to people who already know you — current members, former members, people who've inquired before, the gym owner's personal network, staff networks, and local business contacts.

**The psychology:** Warm outreach converts at 2–5x the rate of cold outreach because the credibility problem is already partially solved. The prospect already has a reference point for who you are.

**Gym-specific applications:**

*Current member warm outreach:*
- "Hey [name], I noticed you haven't been in this week — everything okay? The 7am crew missed you." (Retention as a lead gen mechanism — stops churn before it starts)
- Referral ask: "You've been a member for 6 months and you're doing great. Do you have a friend or coworker who's been thinking about getting started? I'd love to give them your same deal."
- Milestone celebration outreach: "You just hit 50 sessions. Seriously — that's more than most people do in a year. Who do you know that should hear that story?"

*Former member warm outreach:*
- "Hey [name], it's been a few months since we've seen you. I wanted to reach out personally, not with a mass email. We just launched a 30-day Come-Back Program designed specifically for people who've taken a break. Is that something worth 10 minutes to chat about?"
- Do not offer a discount in the first message. Offer connection.

*Inquiry warm outreach (people who asked but didn't join):*
- Segment by time since inquiry: 30 days, 90 days, 6 months, 12 months
- Different message for each segment. The 12-month message acknowledges time passed. The 30-day message is more direct.
- "You reached out a few months ago about the new member program. The timing wasn't right. I don't know if it is now, but we launched something different that might be worth a look — [offer name]."

**Execution cadence:** Warm outreach should run continuously, not in campaigns. Every week: review who has inquired but not joined (last 90 days), who has stopped attending (last 30 days), and who is approaching a membership anniversary.

### Channel 2: Cold Outreach

Reaching out to people who do not know you — through direct mail, cold email, cold DMs, or cold calls. Less efficient per contact than warm outreach, but scalable to a much larger audience.

**The psychology:** Cold outreach fails when it leads with the business. It works when it leads with value or an acute problem the prospect is experiencing.

**Gym-specific applications:**

*Local business partnerships as structured cold outreach:*
- Identify 20 local employers within 2 miles whose employees are the gym's avatar
- Reach out to HR managers or office managers, not CEOs
- Offer: "Employee wellness day — we'll bring a certified trainer to your office for a free 30-minute session for your team. Zero cost, zero commitment. Just a way to introduce what we do."
- This is cold outreach with a strong lead magnet, not a sales pitch

*Neighborhood direct mail — hyperlocal targeting:*
- A 1-mile radius around the gym is the primary capture zone
- New resident lists (people who moved in last 90 days are a strong gym lead)
- Offer-first copy — the envelope and first line must state the offer or result, not the gym name
- Include a personal note signed by the owner — not a mass-printed flyer

*Direct message outreach (use sparingly, high compliance risk):*
- Only to people who have publicly signaled relevant intent: posting about starting a fitness journey, joining local neighborhood groups and mentioning health goals, leaving reviews at competitor gyms
- Message must be relevant to what they shared: "I saw your post about trying to get back into working out. I own [gym name] on [street]. We just launched a program specifically designed for people restarting. Would you want me to send you the details?"
- Never mass DM. Never copy-paste. Never promote without a relevant connection point.

**Execution cadence:** Cold outreach runs in campaigns, not continuously. Direct mail: 4 times per year aligned with seasonal surge periods. Business outreach: 2-3 new partner conversations per month.

### Channel 3: Content

Publishing valuable content to attract strangers who are already in the market, build trust over time with people who are not yet in the market, and create ongoing reasons for warm leads to re-engage.

**The psychology:** Content is the slowest channel to produce results and the most durable once it compounds. A gym that has posted consistently for 12 months in its local market has an asset that compounds indefinitely. A gym that runs ads has leads only as long as it keeps paying.

**Gym-specific applications:**

*Educational content that attracts the avatar:*
- "Three exercises for lower back pain relief you can do at your desk" — targets a pain point the lifestyle member has right now, not a fitness goal they might have later
- "Why most people who try to start working out quit in week 3 — and the one thing that prevents it" — directly addresses the credibility barrier
- "How one of our 47-year-old members went from couch to consistent in 8 weeks without changing her diet" — real story, real person, real result. Not a transformation photo.

*Local community content that builds recognition:*
- "Happy Friday from the [gym name] team — [first name] just hit her 100th session. 100 sessions. That's an identity shift."
- "We asked our members what made them stick this time when they'd quit before. Here's what they said." — social proof as content
- Local event involvement — not just sponsorship, but actual documented participation

*Platform-specific content:* See garyvee-content.md for full platform breakdown.

**Execution cadence:** Minimum one post per day on primary platform. Minimum 3 posts per week on secondary platforms. Content volume matters more than content perfection for local awareness.

### Channel 4: Paid Ads

Paying to distribute content or offers to a targeted audience. Fastest path to leads when an offer and creative are proven. Highest cost per lead when deployed before offer and creative are tested. Runs only with Kai's approval on every campaign and budget item.

**The psychology:** Paid ads amplify what is already working. They do not fix what is broken. A weak offer shown to 100,000 people is still a weak offer. Test with small budgets. Scale what converts.

**Gym-specific applications:**

*Meta (Facebook + Instagram) — primary platform for lifestyle member avatar:*
- Target: women 30–55, within 5 miles, interests in health/wellness/weight loss/family/yoga
- Lead form ads (Facebook native form) convert better than link-to-landing-page for warm audiences
- Video ads outperform static for cold audiences — the first 3 seconds must stop the scroll
- Retargeting (people who watched 50%+ of a video, visited the website, or interacted with the page) converts at 3–5x the rate of cold traffic
- Budget allocation: 70% cold traffic / 30% retargeting (until retargeting audience exceeds 1,000 people — then move to 50/50)

*Google Search — for intent-based leads:*
- Only people who search "gym near me," "personal trainer [city]," or the specific gym name are captured here
- These are Level 4–5 awareness prospects (see schwartz-awareness.md) — they are actively looking
- Google converts at higher intent but lower volume than Meta
- Landing page must match the search term exactly — "gym near me" leads see an offer, not a homepage

*Budget discipline:* Every paid campaign submitted to Kai with: exact dollar amount, exact platform and campaign breakdown, expected CPL based on benchmark data, expected lead volume, expected member conversion at benchmarked close rate. No exceptions. See CLAUDE.md financial rules.

**Execution cadence:** Continuous once Kai has approved a campaign. Review performance weekly. Pause campaigns with CPL > 2x benchmark. Scale campaigns with CPL < 0.5x benchmark.

## The Lead Magnet Framework — MAGIC Criteria

A lead magnet is a free piece of value exchanged for contact information. It must clear all five MAGIC criteria:

**M — Massive desired outcome:** The lead magnet must promise something the prospect genuinely wants. Not "gym tips." "The exact workout plan we give every new member in their first week — free, no strings."

**A — Achieved quickly:** The lead magnet must deliver value fast. A 47-page PDF does not clear this bar. A 1-page "3-exercise morning routine that takes 8 minutes" does. The prospect should be able to use it today.

**G — Gap awareness:** The best lead magnets create awareness of a gap between where the prospect is and where they want to be — without rubbing their face in it. They feel enlightened, not ashamed.

**I — Immediate consumption:** The prospect should be able to consume the entire lead magnet in under 10 minutes. Anything requiring more creates friction before you've established trust.

**C — Consistent with what you sell:** The lead magnet must be the logical first step toward your core offer. A recipe guide builds trust with a nutritionist. A 5-minute morning mobility routine builds trust with a gym.

**Lead magnet formats that work for gyms:**

| Format | Example | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Quick-start guide | "Your First Week at the Gym: A Complete Beginner Checklist" | Removes the intimidation of the unknown |
| Challenge | "7-Day Morning Energy Challenge — 8 minutes, no equipment" | Creates momentum toward membership |
| Quiz | "What's the #1 reason most people quit the gym? Take 2 minutes to find out which one is yours" | Personalizes the entry point, high engagement |
| Mini-training video | "How to do a proper squat without hurting your knees — 4-minute video" | Demonstrates coaching quality before purchase |
| Calculator/assessment | "How many calories are you burning per day? (Most people are shocked by this number)" | Creates gap awareness without shame |
| Local specific guide | "5 Best Morning Walks in [City] Under 2 Miles — Plus One Thing That Multiplies Results" | Community relevance builds local trust |
| Social proof compilation | "What 12 members from [neighborhood] said after their first 30 days" | Builds credibility through specificity |

**What makes a lead magnet irresistible for the lifestyle member:**
1. It acknowledges their history — they've tried before. It doesn't pretend that history doesn't exist.
2. It promises a quick win without a lifestyle overhaul — not "here's how to transform your body" but "here's one thing that will feel different by Friday"
3. It removes a specific fear — not general fitness content but content that addresses what's stopping them right now
4. It requires no commitment and no purchase — the word "free" and "no credit card" are load-bearing copy elements for this avatar

## The Warm Outreach Sequence — Exact Steps and Timing

For gym-specific warm outreach targeting former members and inquiries:

**Day 1 — The reconnection message (text or DM — NOT email):**
"Hey [first name], it's [your name] from [gym]. Wanted to reach out personally — we haven't seen you in a while and I wanted to check in. Hope everything's good on your end."

Do not mention an offer. Do not sell anything. This is a genuine check-in.

**Wait for response.** If no response within 3 days, proceed to Day 4.

**Day 4 — The value message (if no Day 1 response):**
"Hey [name], I know you're busy. I didn't want to send a mass email — wanted to reach out individually. We just launched a [program name] that I think you'd actually like — it's designed specifically for [specific thing relevant to their situation]. No pressure at all. Want me to send you the details?"

**Wait for response.** If no response within 4 days, proceed to Day 8.

**Day 8 — The final touch:**
"[Name], last one from me, I promise. We have [X] spots left in the [program name] — [brief one-line description]. If the timing is right, I'd love to have you back. If not, no worries at all. You can always reach out when you're ready."

Then stop. Do not send a fourth message. Persistence beyond three attempts reads as desperation, not care.

**Conversion benchmarks:**
- Former members: 8–12% response rate, 20–30% of responders book a visit
- Inquiries (30 days old): 15–25% response rate, 30–40% of responders book a visit
- Inquiries (90+ days old): 5–8% response rate, 15–20% of responders book a visit

## The Referral System

Referrals are the highest-quality leads in any gym. They convert faster, pay more reliably, and stay longer. Most gyms underutilize this channel because they ask awkwardly or only once.

**When to ask:**
1. After the first result — the moment a member has a visible win (hit a goal, received a compliment, reached a milestone). This is when their confidence and excitement are highest.
2. At membership renewal — they are re-committing, which is psychological confirmation they value the gym
3. After a coach interaction that went particularly well — strike when relationship equity is highest

**How to ask (exact language):**
"[Name], you've been absolutely crushing it lately. [Specific observation: you've come in 4 times this week / you just hit 50 sessions / you mentioned feeling better in your back]. I know a lot of people who know you are watching what you're doing. Is there anyone in your life — friend, coworker, family member — who you think could use what you've got here? I'd love to bring them in personally and take care of them the way we've taken care of you."

Then be quiet. Let them answer.

**What to offer the referring member:**
- One free month of membership (if they value membership enough to stay, this is high perceived value)
- A personal training session (if they haven't tried it, this opens an upsell path)
- A credit toward gear or supplements (tangible, immediate)
- Public recognition (name on a wall, shoutout in the newsletter — for members who are motivated by community status)

**What to offer the referred lead:**
- Same new member offer as anyone else. Do not create a special "referred friend offer" that differs from your current running offer — it creates confusion and signals that your regular offer is inflated.
- The referrer's endorsement is the real bonus. Lean on it: "Sarah wanted me to personally take care of you. Here's what I'm going to do..."

**Tracking:** Log every referral in intelligence-db/assets/. Record who referred whom, when, which offer converted them, and how long they retained. This data eventually shows your highest-referring member archetypes.

## The Affiliate Framework

Affiliates are local businesses or individuals who send you leads in exchange for compensation. For gyms, this is underutilized but highly effective when structured correctly.

**Who to partner with (high-fit affiliates):**
- Physical therapists and chiropractors — their patients need to continue moving after treatment; gym is the natural next step
- Primary care physicians and nurse practitioners — increasingly asked "what should I do for exercise?" — having a trusted local gym to refer to solves their problem
- Nutritionists and dietitians — diet works better with exercise; gym is a natural complement
- Workplace wellness programs — HR managers at large local employers who want to offer employee benefits
- Sports coaches (youth soccer, adult rec leagues) — their players and athletes need supplemental training
- Real estate agents — they work with new residents who need to build a local community

**How to structure it:**
- Simple, clean, trackable: unique promo code or unique landing page URL per affiliate
- Compensation: $50–$100 per converted member (not per lead — per closed member)
- No upfront cost to the affiliate partner, no ongoing management requirement
- Make it easy for them: provide them with a one-pager they can physically hand to clients, a digital card, and the name of a specific staff member to ask for

**How to approach a potential affiliate:**
"I know your patients often ask what to do for exercise after treatment. We've worked with several of [affiliate type]'s clients in the past — I'd love to be the place you refer them. We have a specific program for [relevant context]. Would it be worth 20 minutes to see if it makes sense for your clients?"

**Tracking:** Every affiliate conversion tracked by promo code or URL. Monthly report to each active affiliate showing how many leads converted. This keeps the relationship warm and demonstrates value.

## The Content Model as Lead Generation

Content does not directly generate leads in most cases. Content generates awareness, trust, and intent — and then a CTA converts that into a lead.

**The content funnel for a local gym:**

Level 1 — Awareness content: Posts that reach people who don't know the gym exists yet. These must be shareable, local, or algorithmically boosted. Pain-point videos, real member stories, surprising statistics.

Level 2 — Credibility content: Posts that prove the gym delivers real results for real people. Not transformation photos — those trigger Meta restrictions and feel manipulative. Real stories, real specificity, real people from the community.

Level 3 — CTA content: Posts with a specific, low-friction next step. "We have 4 spots in the January cohort — [link] to learn more." "Text [name] at [number] if this sounds like what you've been looking for."

The ratio: 80% awareness/credibility content, 20% CTA content. Too many CTAs trains the algorithm and the audience to scroll past. Too few CTAs and the content builds awareness without converting it.

**How strangers become warm leads through content:**
1. Stranger sees pain-point video (Level 1 — doesn't know the gym)
2. Stranger follows the account because the content resonated
3. Over several weeks: credibility content builds trust
4. Stranger sees a specific offer (Level 3)
5. Stranger DMs or clicks — they are now a warm lead

**The timeline:** Average content-to-lead conversion for cold audiences takes 3–6 weeks of consistent exposure. This is why content is not a short-term channel. It requires patience and consistency.

## Which Channel to Start With — No Data, New System

Given that marketing-os is newly initialized with no performance data, no conversion benchmarks, and no established brand presence, the channel priority is:

**Priority 1: Warm outreach (immediate)**
Zero cost. Zero creative production. Requires only a list of former members and inquiries. Even 10 conversions from warm outreach generates cash flow that funds everything else. Start here on Day 1.

**Priority 2: Content (immediate, runs parallel)**
Start publishing daily on the primary platform. The compounding effect begins only when you start. Every day delayed is a day of compounding lost. No data needed — start with problem-aware content for the lifestyle member avatar and adjust based on engagement data within 2 weeks.

**Priority 3: Referral system (start at Week 2)**
Once warm outreach has produced the first new members, install the referral ask system. These first new members are the highest-likelihood referrers — they are excited and fresh.

**Priority 4: Paid ads (start at Week 4–6)**
Only after: (1) an offer has been tested and converted at least once organically, (2) content has been running long enough to have retargeting audiences building, and (3) Kai has approved the specific budget. Do not start paid before offer is validated. Paying to amplify an unvalidated offer is money lost.

**Priority 5: Cold outreach and affiliates (start at Month 2)**
These require infrastructure: business cards, one-pagers, tracking systems, follow-up processes. Build these after the core offer is validated and the team has bandwidth.

**The rule:** Every channel that costs money requires Kai's budget approval. Warm outreach, content, and referrals have zero direct ad spend cost. Start there. Prove the offer. Then invest.
