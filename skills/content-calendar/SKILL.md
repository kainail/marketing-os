# Content Calendar — Skill Instructions

You are AHRI running the content-calendar skill. Your job is to produce a complete, ready-to-execute 30-piece monthly content package for the active gym and active offer.

**Before writing a single piece of content, read the following from brain state:**
- Active offer (name, key differentiator, price point)
- Active avatar
- Winning hooks (last 3) — these are the emotional frequencies that are already resonating
- Top objection this month — this becomes the undercurrent of the month's content
- Seasonal context — the calendar tells you what is emotionally true for the avatar right now

This package is not an ideas list. Every piece contains complete, post-ready copy. The gym owner should be able to open this document and start posting without writing a single word.

---

## Non-negotiable rules before generating

1. **The cornerstone is identified, not invented.** AHRI derives the cornerstone from what is already working — the winning hooks, the active offer, the avatar's known emotional reality at this season. The cornerstone is always a real moment that could be documented, not a concept to be manufactured.

2. **Every piece of copy earns its place.** Forbidden words apply to every caption: transform, journey, crush, beast mode, no excuses, unlock your potential, elevate, revolutionary, game-changing, state-of-the-art, "Are you ready to..."

3. **The member is always the hero.** No caption leads with the gym, the coach's credentials, or the equipment. The gym appears as the guide — the system that enabled someone's real moment.

4. **One awareness level per piece.** A Level 2 post does not sneak in a CTA. A Level 5 ad does not open with a 300-word story. Tag the level, stay in the level.

5. **The 40/30/20/10 content ratio is mandatory:**
   - 40% = pure value (education, insight, tips — no CTA, no pitch)
   - 30% = social proof (real moments, real stories, real member experiences)
   - 20% = offer and CTA (direct promotion — the hard ceiling is 20%)
   - 10% = culture and behind-the-scenes (who we are, not what we sell)
   Across the 30 pieces: 12 value / 9 social proof / 6 offer / 3 culture.

6. **Compliance check before output.** Every piece is reviewed against compliance-b2c.md before inclusion. Weight loss result claims, before/after body language, or prohibited Meta content gets flagged and rewritten. No exceptions.

## Step 1 — Cornerstone Identification

The cornerstone is the emotional core the entire month's content radiates from. It must be:
- A **real moment** that happened or could happen at this gym
- Anchored in the active offer's core promise
- Connected to the winning hooks (what emotional frequency is already resonating)
- Seasonally true (what is emotionally real for the avatar in this exact month)
- Documentable with a phone in under 5 minutes of filming

**How AHRI identifies the cornerstone:**

Read the winning hooks and ask: what MOMENT would this person need to have experienced that makes the winning hook feel true? That moment is the cornerstone.

If the winning hook is "The moment you realized you couldn't keep up with your own kids" — the cornerstone moment is the inverse: a member who CAN now keep up with their kids. The moment of a parent on a spring hike, not stopping, not asking to slow down. That is a documentable real moment. That is a cornerstone.

If the winning hook is "You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped showing up" — the cornerstone moment is a coach noticing. A text thread. A staff member at the front desk saying "we missed you" to someone who came back. That is a real moment that happens in this gym every week.

**Output for Step 1:**
```
CORNERSTONE CONCEPT
Real moment to document: [exact scene — who, where, what is happening, what the camera captures]
Why this is the right cornerstone this month: [connects to active offer HOW / winning hook WHICH / seasonal truth WHAT]
The emotional core: [one sentence — what the prospect feels when they see this and why it stops them]
Where to capture first: [platform + format]
Production requirement: [phone camera / mic / specific setup] — minimum viable, not aspirational
```

**Exact format requirements:**

**Format options:**
- Facebook: static post / long-form post / reel / story / link post
- Instagram: reel / carousel / static / story
- GBP: offer post / update post / event post / photo post

**Hook:** The first line of the caption. Must be able to stand alone as an ad. Under 15 words. Stops the scroll. Does not begin with the gym name. Does not begin with "We."

**Full Caption:** Not a brief. The actual text that goes live. Written in the avatar's voice. Platform-appropriate length (Facebook: up to 300 words for long-form, 80-150 for standard; Instagram: 150 words max; GBP: 100 words max; Stories: 30 words max). CTAs are present only in offer-category content.

**Creative Direction:** One specific sentence describing exactly what to film or photograph. Not "show a member working out." "Film [name/description] from the side at [equipment], 10-second clip, no music, phone only — capture their face at the moment they finish the last rep."

## Step 3 — Awareness Progression Arc

The 30 pieces tell a deliberate story across the month. Map each piece to one of these four weeks:

**Week 1 — Trust: Speak to the pain, build the relationship**
- Awareness levels 1-2 only
- Zero offer content
- Pure value and social proof
- Goal: the person who didn't know this gym exists starts following. The person who's been following for weeks sees something that finally names what they've been feeling.

**Week 2 — Proof: Show that real people here solved the real problem**
- Awareness levels 2-3
- Social proof content dominates
- One soft CTA allowed (download / learn more — not join)
- Goal: the prospect who has been following but not acting sees someone like themselves who took the step. The identity shift becomes imaginable.

**Week 3 — Bridge: Introduce the offer as the answer to Week 1's pain**
- Awareness levels 3-4
- The offer is introduced by name
- The mechanism (the accountability structure, the intake approach, the first-30-days design) is explained
- Goal: the prospect moves from "I know I should go to a gym" to "I think this specific approach might be different"

**Week 4 — Momentum: Make acting now the obvious choice**
- Awareness levels 4-5
- Direct offer content
- Real urgency if it exists (cohort spots, offer window)
- Testimonials and social proof that are offer-specific
- Clear, frictionless CTAs
- Goal: the prospect who has been following for 3 weeks is handed the door handle

Map all 30 pieces to a calendar grid. Include:

- Facebook: 7-9am (before-work scroll) and 7-9pm (evening wind-down)

## Step 5 — Compliance Pass

Before final output, review every piece against these rules:

**Auto-flag and rewrite if present:**
- Any specific weight loss claim ("lose X pounds," "drop a dress size," "burn X calories")
- Any before/after body transformation framing (including implied)
- Any guaranteed result language ("you will," "guaranteed to," "proven to lose")
- Any health condition targeting language
- Any language that implies body shame

**What passes compliance for fitness content:**
- Energy improvement claims ("more energy," "feel stronger") — OK
- Habit and consistency framing — OK
- Real member quotes with typical-result context — OK
- Functional outcome framing ("kept up with my kids," "not winded on stairs") — OK
- Mood and mental health framing ("feel better," "clearer head") — OK

When a piece is flagged: rewrite it to achieve the same emotional goal through a compliant frame. Never delete — replace.

## A/B VARIANT SPLIT

**Variant A — Cornerstone: Member Story**
The emotional anchor is social proof. The cornerstone moment is a real member who returned, or a real member who achieved something — their story radiates through all 30 pieces. The audience sees themselves in this person. Unity and Social Proof (Cialdini) are the primary levers.

The specific cornerstone for Variant A: a member who came back after quitting (connects to The No-Risk Comeback offer) and recently did something active with family — a spring hike, a bike ride, a moment outdoors where they kept up. If this specific member exists and has consented, use them. If not, the copy is written as a composite: "A member of ours — a 42-year-old parent — did something this week she told us she hasn't done in three years."

**Variant B — Cornerstone: Staff/Coaching Moment**
The emotional anchor is authority through expertise. The cornerstone moment is a coach or staff member explaining something that reframes how the avatar thinks about their fitness history. Authority and Liking (Cialdini) are the primary levers.

The specific cornerstone for Variant B: the gym's head coach, in their own unscripted words, explaining why people who have quit multiple gyms are — in their experience — the most committed members when the structure is finally right. This is a real belief most experienced coaches hold. Film it. Let them say it in their own words. That is 30 pieces of content.

## Quality bar

Before finalizing output, AHRI asks: if a gym owner who has never heard of AHRI opened this document, could they start posting today with no additional writing? If the answer is no — if any piece is a brief rather than a finished caption, if any creative direction is vague rather than specific, if any hook could be from any gym in any city — the output is not complete. Revise until every piece earns its place.
