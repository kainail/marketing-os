The moment you realized you couldn't keep up with your own kids.

The people who figure this out don't have more discipline than you. They just found a way to make showing up automatic instead of optional.

There's a way to get there that doesn't require willpower every single morning.

If you've been thinking about it, this is worth reading.

**CHARACTER COUNT:** 33 ✓

**WORD COUNT:** 17 ✓

**CTA BUTTON:** Learn More

**CREATIVE DIRECTION:**
Shot type: Photo or 20-second silent video with text overlay
Subject: A parent — 38–50, dressed in regular weekend clothes, not athletic wear — standing still while a child (7–12 years old) runs ahead on a trail, a sidewalk, or across a park. The parent isn't collapsed or visibly distressed. Just stopped. Watching. There is love in the look, and also something quieter underneath it.
Setting: Outdoors. Natural light. Late afternoon, golden hour preferred. Nothing staged. This should look like a phone photo someone actually took.
Mood/lighting: Warm but slightly tired. Real. The emotional weight is in the stillness of the parent, not in dramatic contrast.
Text overlay (if video): Hook text appears on screen at the 3-second mark. White text, clean sans-serif, no animation effects. Stays for 4 seconds, then fades. No other text overlays.
What the image must NOT show: Gym equipment. Fitness clothing brands. Anyone visibly overweight in a context that reads as body shaming. Before/after framing of any kind. A person who looks unhealthy or ill. Any imagery that implies the parent is failing — the tone is recognition, not judgment.

- [x] No countdown timers specified that are not genuine
- [x] No health condition targeting described
- [x] Landing page delivers exactly what this ad promises — landing page must speak to the energy/habit problem before introducing the offer; cannot open with "$1 trial" headline

**TARGETING BRIEF:**

Placement: Facebook Feed (primary). Instagram Feed (secondary). Do not run in Stories for this creative — the stillness of the image requires dwell time.
Exclusions: Current gym members (custom audience). Anyone who has submitted a lead form in the last 30 days. Anyone who has visited the website in the last 7 days (those move to retargeting).
Estimated audience size: Medium — 80,000–250,000 depending on radius. Broad enough for the algorithm to optimize, tight enough to stay relevant.

## AD TESTING MATRIX

Run one variable at a time. Declare a winner with statistical confidence before moving to the next variable. Never test multiple variables simultaneously.

| Round | Variable | Control (Variant A) | Challenger (Variant B) | Ad Set | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hook | "You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped." | "The moment you realized you couldn't keep up with your own kids." | Cold | CTR — which hook stops the scroll. Minimum 1,000 impressions per variant, 72 hours minimum at $20+/day. |
| 2 | Headline | "You're not lazy. You never had support." | "When did keeping up get hard?" | Cold (winning hook from Round 1) | CTR — headline read in conjunction with winning hook creative. Same budget and duration requirements. |
| 3 | Creative | Parent/kids static image — stillness, outdoor | Coach/member interaction inside gym — relationship visible | Cold (winning hook + winning headline) | CTR and CPL — creative determines who clicks and at what cost. |

Note: Warm and retargeting ad sets are built on the winning cold hook. Do not test new variables in warm and retargeting until the cold winner is confirmed — the cold audience provides the largest sample size and the cleanest data. Minimum run time before declaring any winner: 72 hours at $20+/day spend per variant. Minimum sample: 1,000 impressions per variant. CPL is the primary success metric for retargeting. CTR is the primary metric for cold and warm until lead volume is sufficient to optimize on CPL directly.
