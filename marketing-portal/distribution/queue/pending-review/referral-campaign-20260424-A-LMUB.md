# SECTION 3: INCENTIVE MODEL ANALYSIS AND RECOMMENDATION

---

## MODEL A: REFERRER-ONLY REWARD

**What the referring member receives:** One free month of membership (highest perceived value for an active member — they value what they are already paying for)

**What the referred friend receives:** Standard No-Risk Comeback offer — $1 / 30 days coached. Same as any new member.

**Scoring:**

| Dimension | Score | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Believability | 8/10 | One free month is a concrete, tangible reward with clear value. Member can calculate what they're getting. |
| Referrer motivation | 7/10 | Strong for members who are budget-conscious. Less motivating for members who have fully internalized the value and don't think about cost. |
| Cost to gym per conversion | ~$54 per referred member who joins | One month's membership revenue forgone. At $975 LTV, this is a 5.5% acquisition cost — well below the $100 CAC target. |
| Likelihood of execution | 7/10 | Staff needs to follow through on the reward reliably. If the reward is applied inconsistently, trust breaks. GHL must automate the reward trigger to remove human failure points. |
| **Total score** | **29/40** | |

---

## MODEL B: REFERRED-FRIEND-ONLY REWARD

**What the referring member receives:** Nothing material. The act of helping a friend is framed as the complete reward.

**What the referred friend receives:** Enhanced offer — $1 / 30 days coached + one complimentary personal training session (not available publicly). Value: $60–$100.

**Scoring:**

| Dimension | Score | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Believability | 9/10 | No transaction feels cleaner. The endorsement is clearly genuine when there is nothing in it for the referrer. |
| Referrer motivation | 5/10 | Requires strong community identity to motivate action without a tangible reward. Works well for highly engaged members. Underperforms for members who haven't yet built that identity. |
| Cost to gym per conversion | ~$60–$100 per referred member who joins | Cost of the complimentary PT session. However: PT intro is the single best upsell on-ramp. This cost may return 3x in PT package revenue. |
| Likelihood of execution | 6/10 | Depends entirely on the member genuinely wanting to help. Cannot be prompted with the same directness. Staff script must be softer and therefore less consistent. |
| **Total score** | **25/40** | |

---

## MODEL C: BOTH REWARDED (SMALLER REWARDS)

**What the referring member receives:** $25 account credit (applied to next billing cycle)

**What the referred friend receives:** Standard No-Risk Comeback offer + first week of access waived (they pay $1 but their clock starts at day 8, giving them a soft extra week)

**Scoring:**

| Dimension | Score | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Believability | 7/10 | Two-sided incentives are familiar (think: Uber referral). Consumers understand the model. Risk: feels transactional rather than community-driven. |
| Referrer motivation | 6/10 | $25 credit is meaningful but less exciting than a full free month. Smaller reward, smaller motivation. |
| Cost to gym per conversion | ~$25 account credit + 1 week of deferred revenue per referred join | Lowest direct cost per conversion but lowest emotional impact. |
| Likelihood of execution | 7/10 | Easy to understand, easy to communicate, easy to automate. Simplicity improves execution reliability. |
| **Total score** | **26/40** | |

---

## AHRI RECOMMENDATION: MODEL A

**Recommended model:** Model A — Referrer-only reward (one free month)

**Reasoning:**

The lifestyle member avatar's dominant Cialdini affinities are Unity and Commitment/Consistency. At first glance, this might suggest Model B (identity-forward, community-driven). But there is a sequencing problem: the identity of "someone who belongs here and cares about this community" develops over months, not days. At the 14-day trigger — the earliest point in this system — most members have not yet fully internalized that identity. They are still in the phase of proving to themselves that they will stick with it.

Model A works at every trigger point regardless of how developed the member's community identity is, because the reward makes the ask feel reciprocal rather than extractive. The member is not being asked to do something for the gym. They are being offered a thank-you for doing something for a friend. That framing is both motivating and honest.

The one free month is the right reward specifically because it is high perceived value (the member already values what they are paying for), zero cost to administer (it is forgiven revenue, not cash out), and it deepens the member's own commitment to the gym (they now have an extra month invested — Commitment/Consistency activates for the referrer as well as the referee).

At Anytime Fitness's LTV of ~$975 per standard member, a $54 acquisition cost via referral is the most efficient channel in the entire system. Model A should be the default from day one.

**When to shift to Model B:** At approximately 6 months of program operation, when a meaningful cohort of members has reached the 6-month and 12-month anniversary triggers, run a 30-day A/B test of Model A vs. Model B on Triggers 5 and 6 only (the highest-identity-engagement triggers). If Model B conversion rate within 10% of Model A, shift those triggers to Model B — the qualitative referral quality will improve even if volume holds.

**[KAI — before implementing Model A, confirm with your Anytime Fitness franchise agreement that offering a free month as a referral incentive does not conflict with AF pricing policies. Confirm the GHL automation for applying the free month credit to the referring member's billing cycle is built before the first trigger fires. A referral reward promised and not delivered is worse than no referral program at all.]**

## Attribution Logic

**When a referred friend submits the landing page form:**
1. GHL creates a new contact with tag `referred-lead`
2. `Referred_by` field is populated from the unique link parameter (each member's link carries their contact ID)
3. `Referral_trigger` field is populated from the link parameter (trigger 1–6 encoded in URL)
4. `Referral_date` is logged automatically

**When the referred lead books a visit:**
Standard GHL nurture sequence begins (Jessica). The `referred-lead` tag passes context to the nurture sequence — Jessica's sequence for referred leads can be a lighter-touch version since pre-existing trust is already established.

**When the referred lead converts to membership:**
1. Tag `successful-referral` applied to the referring member's record
2. GHL task created: "Apply free month credit — [Referring member name]" — assigned to Kai or front desk
3. Note added to referring member's record: "Successful referral — [referred member name] — [date]"
4. Monthly summary task auto-created on the first of each month: "Review referral conversions — identify top referrers" — assigned to Kai

# SECTION 5: IMPLEMENTATION NOTE FOR KAI

Before a single trigger fires, one thing needs to be true: every staff member who has any member-facing interaction must have the Trigger 6 script memorized.

Not printed and posted. Not emailed in a PDF. Memorized.

Trigger 6 is the highest-intent moment in this entire system — a member says something unprompted and genuine, and the window to respond is approximately 90 seconds. If the staff member has to think about what to say, the moment is gone. If they feel awkward or uncertain, the member feels the awkwardness and the moment is gone. The only way this works is if the response is so natural and so practiced that it feels like something a person would genuinely say in that moment — because with enough practice, it will be.

Run a 15-minute training session before launch. Say the scripts out loud. Practice the follow-ups. Specifically rehearse what it feels like to say "who in your life needs to feel this way?" in the middle of a normal conversation. The staff member who can do this without hesitation is the single most valuable asset in this referral system. Everything else — the landing page, the GHL setup, the incentive structure — is infrastructure. This is the activation.
