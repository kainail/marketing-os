# SECTION 1: CORNERSTONE CONCEPT BRIEF — VARIANT B

```
CORNERSTONE CONCEPT — VARIANT B

Real moment to document:
The head coach, seated or standing casually in the gym — not behind a desk, not
in front of a camera setup — talking directly into a phone camera in their own
words. No script. The topic: "The members who've quit three gyms before joining
here are, in my experience, the most committed people in this building — and here's
why." The camera captures their face, their hands when they gesture, the gym
floor visible behind them. Natural light if possible. This is a 4–6 minute
unscripted conversation filmed in one take.

Why this is the right cornerstone this month:
Active offer = The No-Risk Comeback — a program explicitly named for people who
have tried and stopped. The winning hook "You're not lazy. You've just never had
anyone notice when you stopped showing up" is a coaching belief, not a marketing
claim. It lands hardest in the coach's voice, not in ad copy. The seasonal truth
of late April is motion — the world outside is moving, routines are reforming,
and people who paused over winter are making decisions about whether this spring
is different. A coach speaking with genuine conviction about why the "I've quit
before" history is not a liability but a signal of the right kind of person — that
reframes the prospect's self-story at exactly the moment they need it reframed.

The emotional core:
A person who has failed at gyms before watches this and thinks: "He's talking
about me — and he's saying I'm not broken." That is the stop-scroll moment.

Where to capture first:
Instagram Reel (60–90 seconds, cut from the longer recording) — discovery
platform, reaches cold and warm audiences equally, algorithmic distribution
to non-followers.

Production requirement:
One phone, propped or handheld. Coach standing or seated — casual, not staged.
Natural gym background. No ring light, no production setup. The rawness is
intentional. 10 minutes to film, 30 minutes to edit into the derivative cuts.
```
