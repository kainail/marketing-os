## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

**Lead with Variant B.**

The No-Risk Comeback's specific differentiator is structural — the coached check-in system built around weeks two and three, the exact window when the lifestyle member has historically stopped. That mechanism is not just a selling point. It is a direct answer to the primary objection this avatar carries into every gym decision they make: *"I've tried this before and quit."*

Variant B's newness frame makes that mechanism the lead. Every touchpoint opens with the question: *"What is different now?"* — and answers it with something specific and credible. For a cancelled member at 60, 90, or 180 days, the emotional math is: *the reason I stopped before may not apply anymore.* That is a legitimate reopening of the decision, not a guilt trip and not a discount.

The spring timing reinforces this. Late April is a natural inflection point — not January resolution energy, but a quieter, more considered "I should actually do this" moment that fits the newness frame better than warmth alone. This avatar responds to specificity and credibility over sentimentality when they are reconsidering a decision they have made before. Give them a reason. Variant B gives them a reason.

Variant A (warmth primary) is the right secondary variant for the 12-month email specifically — by that point, the relationship frame matters more than the information frame, and the emotional weight of a genuine goodbye benefits from warmth as the lead. Consider running Variant A for the 12-month window only and Variant B for windows 1 through 3.
