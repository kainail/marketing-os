# The No-Risk Comeback — 30 Days Coached, $1 to Start
## Try the gym that was built for people who've quit before. If it doesn't work, you're out a dollar and nothing else.

---

### A/B Variant Angle

Variant B leads with risk removal. The Lifestyle Member's deepest, most paralyzing barrier is not money, time, or knowledge — it is the fear of failing again. Every previous gym membership that ended in cancellation left a scar on their self-concept. This version of the offer is structured so that every single element answers one question: "What if I try this and it doesn't work?" The framing makes saying yes feel safer than continuing to wait. The $1 price point, the guarantee, the accountability structure, and the explicit naming of past failure as a system problem (not a personal one) all exist to collapse the emotional risk to near zero. The prospect should finish reading this offer and think: "I literally have nothing to lose — and something is clearly different here."

### The Guarantee

**Guarantee Option A — Outcome Guarantee:**
Complete 12 sessions in your first 30 days. If you don't feel noticeably more energetic and stronger than the day you started — tell us. Full refund of your $1, and you keep the workout plan, the 30-minute library, and everything your coach built for you. No questions, no awkward conversation, no fine print.

**Guarantee Option B — Effort Guarantee:**
Show up 12 times in 30 days. If you do — and you genuinely feel like this isn't working for you — we refund your dollar and give you a free month at the gym of your choice. We'll even help you find one. That's how confident we are that showing up here, with this system, is different from anything you've tried.

**Recommendation: Guarantee Option A.**

For this avatar on this variant, Option A is stronger. Here's why: the Lifestyle Member's deepest fear is not losing money — it's investing emotional hope and being disappointed. Option A directly addresses that fear by tying the guarantee to the subjective experience of feeling better, not just to showing up. "If you don't feel it, you get everything back" tells the prospect: *we are betting our money on the idea that this works for you.* That confidence transfer is the single most powerful trust-builder available at Level 5 awareness.

Option B is creative and bold, but the "we'll help you find another gym" element introduces complexity and may feel performative to a skeptical prospect. Option A is cleaner, more direct, and easier to believe.

### Deployment Notes

**One-sentence offer summary:** We help people who've tried gyms and quit build a consistent habit in 30 days — fully coached, $1 to start — without needing willpower, experience, or the fear of failing again.

**Recommended awareness level for paid ads:** Level 5 — Most Aware. This offer is built for prospects who already know the gym, have visited the site or engaged with content, and need the final push to act. For Level 3–4, the risk-removal framing still works but should be paired with mechanism-forward hooks that explain *why* this is different before presenting the full offer.

**Strongest avatar segments:** Primary — Lifestyle Member, 38–52, who has cancelled at least one previous gym membership. Secondary — any prospect in the retargeting audience (website visitors, video 75%+ viewers, lead form openers who didn't convert). This offer is specifically designed for the "I've been burned before" psychology.

**What to build next:**
1. **Hook-writer** — Generate Level 5 hooks for this offer. Lead hooks should emphasize the risk-removal angle: "tried before and quit" language, the $1 entry, and the accountability mechanism. Test pain-point hooks ("If you've joined a gym and quit within the first month...") against curiosity hooks ("There's one thing most gyms leave out — and it's the reason you stopped going").
2. **Ad copy** — Facebook/Instagram retargeting ads for website visitors and video viewers. Short-form (Level 5, direct offer) and medium-form (Level 4–5, mechanism + offer).
3. **Landing page** — Full offer landing page with value stack, guarantee, 3–5 real testimonials from former quitters who stayed, FAQ section addressing top 4 objections, and single CTA to claim the $1 trial.
4. **Email nurture sequence** — For leads who submit the form but don't complete signup. 4-email sequence over 7 days, each addressing one ranked objection, ending with urgency from cohort capacity.
