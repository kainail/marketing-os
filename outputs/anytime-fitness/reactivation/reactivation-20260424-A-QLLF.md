## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

**Lead with Variant A.**

The active offer — The No-Risk Comeback — is emotionally framed around a fresh start and a genuine accountability structure. Its core promise is not a result; it is a different experience. That is a warmth and trust argument, not a newness argument. Variant A's warmth-first approach earns the credibility to make that case before the offer lands. For a cancelled member who left because the system didn't hold them — not because the gym was bad, not because the offer was wrong — the most disarming thing you can send is a message that doesn't feel like a reactivation campaign at all.

The seasonal context also favors this. Late April is a moment when people are aware they let the January motivation dissolve. The emotional temperature is quiet guilt, not active urgency. A message that meets them with warmth instead of another pitch is more likely to be opened, more likely to generate a reply, and more likely to produce the conversation that converts. Variant B's newness frame is the stronger test for the 6-month and 12-month windows — when enough time has passed that a genuine "things are different now" argument holds weight. At 60 and 90 days, warmth is the right lead.
