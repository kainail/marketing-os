## SECTION 2: REFERRAL LANDING PAGE BRIEF

**Page URL structure:** [gym-domain]/friend — clean, memorable, easy to text

**Page purpose:** Convert a referred friend into a booked intro visit or trial enrollment. One job. One form. One outcome.

---

**Element 1 — The Attribution Line (above the fold, first thing seen)**

> "[Member first name] thought you'd fit in here."

Not the gym name. Not a logo. Not a headline about fitness. The name of the person they trust is the first thing they read. This is the entire reason they clicked.

Design note: [Member first name] is dynamically pulled from the GHL referral link. If dynamic population fails, fallback: "Someone you trust thought you'd fit in here."

---

**Element 2 — The Social Proof Block**

One real member quote. Sourced from GHL member notes, review collection, or post-30-day check-in surveys. Selected for maximum relevance to the lifestyle member identity.

Quote format:
> "I've joined four gyms in the last ten years and quit every single one. I've been going here for nine months. I genuinely don't know how to explain what's different except that someone actually notices when I'm not there."
> — Karen, member since March

Selection criteria: Quote must come from someone who identifies with the "tried before and stopped" experience. Physical transformation language is not used in the quote. The result described is consistency, energy, or identity — not body composition.

[KAI — before placing any real member quote on this page, get explicit written consent from that member. A simple text asking "We'd love to use your quote on our referral page — totally optional, just your first name and member-since date" is sufficient. Log consent in GHL.]

Compliance note: This quote describes a subjective, individual experience. No typical result implication. No FTC disclosure required beyond standard "individual experience" framing if that language is incorporated naturally.

---

**Element 3 — The Offer Block**

**The No-Risk Comeback**
$1 for your first 30 days.

What's included:
- Full gym access, 24/7
- Guided onboarding session with a coach in your first week
- Two personal check-ins during the 30 days
- No contract. No pressure.

If it's not right for you after 30 days, you owe nothing more.

Design note: Price is presented after value. The bullet list establishes what $1 buys before the dollar amount is repeated. The guarantee line is the last thing they read before the form.

---

**Element 4 — The Form**

One field: phone number.

Label: "Your number — a real person will call you within one business day."

Button text: "I'm interested"

[Do not use: "Sign up now," "Join today," "Get started," "Claim your spot." These phrases trigger the commitment resistance of a prospect who has not yet decided. "I'm interested" is a lower-stakes micro-commitment that converts higher for this avatar.]

---

**Element 5 — The Reassurance Line**

Directly below the button:

> "We'll call you personally — a real person, not a robot — within one business day. No sales pressure. Just a quick conversation to see if this is a fit."

This line is load-bearing for this avatar. The fear of being dropped into an automated sequence is real. Naming it and neutralizing it removes the last hesitation before they submit.

[KAI — this line only works if it is true. The call must happen within one business day, every time, from a real person. If GHL automation fires an SMS first, the SMS must also be warm and non-automated in tone. The promise made on the landing page must be kept exactly as stated. If staff bandwidth cannot support same-business-day calls, change the line to "within two business days" — but do not make a promise you cannot keep.]

---

**Element 6 — Page Constraints**

- No header navigation
- No footer links except privacy policy (required — CAN-SPAM and FTC best practice)
- No exit pop-ups
- No countdown timers unless a genuine cohort scarcity date exists and the cohort is actually filling
- No stock photography — gym interior photo or real member action shot only
- Mobile-first layout — this page is reached via a text link, therefore viewed on a phone

Privacy policy link: Required. Place in page footer. Small but visible. Links to gym's actual privacy policy.

## SECTION 3: INCENTIVE MODEL ANALYSIS AND RECOMMENDATION

---

**Model A: Referrer-Only Reward**

Referring member receives: One free month of membership
Referred friend receives: Standard active offer only ($1 / 30-day trial)

**Scoring:**

- Believability (1–10): **8** — A free month is simple, tangible, and directly tied to something the member already values. No currency conversion, no redemption complexity.
- Referrer motivation (1–10): **7** — A free month is high perceived value for a member who values their membership. Slightly lower motivation score because the reward is deferred — they receive it after the friend converts, not at the moment of the ask.
- Cost to gym per conversion: **~$54.17** (one free month at standard rate) + **$1 trial loss** from referred friend's first month. Offset by the referred friend's projected LTV of ~$975.
- Likelihood of execution (1–10): **7** — Staff must remember to apply the credit. Requires a GHL automation trigger to add the free month to the referring member's account when the referred lead converts. If this automation is not set up, staff will forget and the reward loses credibility.
- **Total score: 29/40**

---

**Model B: Referred-Friend-Only Reward**

Referring member receives: Nothing material — the act of helping a friend is the reward
Referred friend receives: Enhanced version of active offer not publicly available — suggested: $1 trial + one complimentary PT intro session (not part of standard offer)

**Scoring:**

- Believability (1–10): **9** — A better deal for the friend feels genuinely generous rather than transactional. The member is doing something good for someone they care about, not earning a discount.
- Referrer motivation (1–10): **5** — No material incentive for the referrer. Motivation depends entirely on relationship quality and community identity. Works well for established gym culture; weaker for new gyms still building that identity.
- Cost to gym per conversion: **~$0 direct reward cost to referrer** + cost of complimentary PT session (coach time, typically 30–45 min = $30–45 at mid-market PT rate). Offset by referred friend LTV and PT upsell potential from the intro session.
- Likelihood of execution (1–10): **8** — Simpler for staff. No credit to apply. The ask itself is the mechanism.
- **Total score: 27/40**

---

**Model C: Both Rewarded (Smaller Rewards)**

Referring member receives: $25 account credit (applicable to PT sessions or merchandise)
Referred friend receives: $1 trial + one complimentary group orientation session (not standard)

**Scoring:**

- Believability (1–10): **7** — Both parties receiving something feels balanced, but the $25 credit for the referrer introduces a transactional frame that slightly undermines the "doing something for a friend" positioning.
- Referrer motivation (1–10): **6** — $25 credit is meaningful but not as psychologically satisfying as a free month. Feels like a coupon rather than a genuine thank-you.
- Cost to gym per conversion: **~$25 credit + group orientation time** (~30 min coach time). Lowest direct cost of the three models.
- Likelihood of execution (1–10): **7** — Credit must be applied accurately. Two separate rewards to track vs. Model A's one.
- **Total score: 26/40**

---

**AHRI Recommendation: Model A — Referrer-Only Reward (Free Month)**

For Anytime Fitness at current stage — a gym in early growth, establishing its community, running its first referral system — Model A is the correct starting point.

Here is the reasoning: The lifestyle member avatar's top Cialdini affinity is Unity, but Unity requires an established community to activate. That community is still being built. At this stage, the member's identity as "someone who belongs here" is 30–90 days old at best. The intrinsic motivation that powers Model B — "I want my friends to experience what I have" — is present but fragile. It has not yet compounded into the kind of community pride that generates spontaneous referrals without material incentive.

Model A bridges that gap. The free month reward gives the member a concrete, tangible reason to act on a referral impulse they may already have but haven't acted on. The framing — "we want to thank you for thinking of your friends" — preserves the emotional frame of generosity while removing the activation barrier. Over time, as community identity strengthens, you may find that Model B outperforms. At that point, AHRI will flag the data and recommend the transition.

The free month reward also has a second-order effect that the other models lack: it keeps the referring member active for an additional month at no cost to them, reducing the probability of churn during what is often the "post-honeymoon" softening period (months 2–4). The reward is simultaneously an acquisition mechanism and a retention mechanism.

**One score above all others to watch:** Likelihood of execution — scored 7 for Model A specifically because the reward requires accurate, timely application of a free month credit. If this automation is not set up in GHL before launch, the score drops to 4 and the program credibility collapses on its first failure. Set up the automation before the first ask is made.

**Attribution Logic**

When a referred friend submits the landing page form:
1. GHL logs the referral source URL (trigger-specific URL identifies which trigger fired)
2. Referred_by field is populated from the URL parameter carrying the referring member's contact ID
3. Referral_trigger field is populated from the URL parameter (1–6)
4. Tag referred-lead is applied to the new lead
5. Tag referring-member is applied to the source member's record if not already present

When the referred lead converts to a paying member:
1. Referral_converted field = Yes
2. Referral_conversion_date is populated
3. Tag successful-referral is applied to referring member's record
4. GHL automation fires: free month credit is applied to referring member's account
5. If referring member now has 2+ successful referrals, vip-referrer tag is applied
6. A task is created in GHL assigned to [KAI] for personal outreach to the referring member

---

**Monthly Reporting Routine**

At the start of each month, AHRI generates a referral summary from GHL data:

- Total referral links sent (by trigger)
- Total referred leads generated (by trigger)
- Total referring leads converted to members (by trigger)
- Conversion rate by trigger (referred leads → members)
- Top referring members (by successful referral count)
- Cost per referred member acquisition (free months applied ÷ conversions)

This report feeds intelligence-db/assets/referral-performance.json and is reviewed monthly by Kai before the following month's content calendar is set.

**The data question this tracking answers within 90 days:** Which of the 6 triggers generates the highest-quality referred leads — not just the most leads, but the ones who actually convert and stay. This informs where coaching staff should focus their attention and where referral ask training matters most.

---

**Top Referrer Identification Protocol**

After 90 days of program running:
1. Pull VIP Referrers smart list
2. Sort by successful referral count descending
3. Review the top 5 members' records — note their tenure, avatar alignment, trigger type that generated their referrals
4. Flag to Kai for personal recognition (see Section 5)
5. Add a note to each VIP referrer's record identifying them as a community anchor — this informs retention priority if they ever show reduced attendance

## SECTION 5: IMPLEMENTATION NOTE FOR KAI

The single most important thing you need to do to make this system run is also the one thing it cannot automate: **call or personally text every VIP referrer within 48 hours of their second successful referral.**

Not an automated email. Not a GHL sequence. You, personally, with their name and the specific thing they did.

"Hey [name] — I saw that two people you sent our way actually joined and are doing great. I just wanted to call and say thank you personally. That kind of thing matters more than you know. We're lucky to have people like you here."

That call takes three minutes. What it does is disproportionate to its length. It tells a member — who already cares enough about your gym to send two friends — that the owner of the gym noticed them specifically. That is the highest form of Unity you can deliver. It converts a satisfied member into an advocate who will keep referring without any incentive, because they now have a personal relationship with the person who built this place.

The GHL automation will handle everything else — the free month credit, the tags, the smart list, the monthly report. The automation cannot replicate what happens when you make that call. Everything AHRI builds here creates the conditions for that moment. The moment itself is yours.
