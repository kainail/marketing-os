## MOMENT 1 — After the Orientation Session

**Why this moment:**
The orientation session removes the fear that kept this person from walking through the door for months — sometimes years. The lifestyle member came in expecting to feel lost, judged, or overwhelmed. They are leaving having been shown exactly where to go, what to do, and that someone knows their name. The relief is physical. This is the moment when "I'm not sure this is for me" becomes "I think I can actually do this." That emotional shift deserves to be captured — and the member, in this moment, is genuinely motivated to tell someone about it.

**In-person script (Variant B — orientation):**
"I have to say — you asked really good questions in there. That's exactly the kind of thing that makes a difference in the first few weeks. ## MOMENT 2 — After First Month Completion

**Why this moment:**
Thirty days. For the lifestyle member, this is the wall they have never gotten past. Every previous gym attempt ended here — around week 3 or 4, when the novelty wore off and the habit hadn't formed yet. The fact that they are still here is not small. It is the thing they told themselves they couldn't do. The coach who names that truth — specifically, not generically — is handing them something real. The review that comes from this moment is not "great gym." It is the review that describes what it felt like to be the person who quit every time, and then didn't. That review is worth more to a skeptical prospect than ten five-star one-liners.

# SECTION 2: THE 8 RESPONSE TEMPLATES

---

### Template 1 — 5-Star Enthusiastic (Detailed Review)
**When to use:** Reviewer wrote multiple sentences. Named a staff member, described a specific experience, or shared a personal outcome.
**Personalization note:** Pull one specific detail from their review and name it. Never respond generically to a detailed review — the effort they put in deserves a reply that proves you actually read it.
**Under 75 words.**

**Variation A:**
"[Reviewer name] — thank you for taking the time to write this. What you said about [specific detail from their review] is exactly what we're trying to create here, and it genuinely means something to read it in your words. We're glad you're part of this. See you [on Tuesday / this week / soon]."

**Variation B:**
"This made our morning, [Reviewer name]. [Specific detail they mentioned] — that's not something we take for granted. Thank you for sharing that honestly. It's why we do this work. We'll see you soon."

**Variation C:**
"[Reviewer name], what you wrote about [specific detail] is something our coaches will actually read this week. Thank you for putting it into words. We're grateful you're here, and we don't say that lightly."

---

### Template 2 — 5-Star Brief (One or Two Words from Reviewer)
**When to use:** "Great gym!" / "Love it!" / "5 stars" — minimal text, maximum stars.
**Approach:** Warmer than the review earned. Makes them feel seen. Invites them without pressure to say more next time.
**Under 50 words.**
**Personalization note:** If their name is visible, use it. If their profile shows any clues about their situation, reference it obliquely. Otherwise, keep it warm and human.

"Thank you — we genuinely appreciate every single one of these. If there's ever something specific that stood out during your time with us that you'd want to share, we'd love to hear it. Until then: glad you're here."

---

### Template 3 — 4-Star (Good Experience, Implied Gap)
**When to use:** The reviewer had a real experience, they valued it enough to leave a review, and something didn't quite reach their standard. They may or may not name it.
**Under 75 words.**
**Personalization note:** Do not be defensive about the missing star. Do not explain it away. Treat it as honest feedback from someone invested enough in the gym to tell you something.

"[Reviewer name] — thank you for this. Four stars from someone who clearly cares about the experience means we did something right and left something on the table. I'd genuinely like to know what that something is. Would you be open to me reaching out directly? You can email me at [owner email] or just reply here. I want to get this right."

---

### Template 4 — 3-Star or Below (Negative Experience)
**When to use:** 1, 2, or 3 stars. The experience was genuinely bad by their account.
**Under 100 words.**
**Personalization note:** Read their review twice before responding. Pull the specific concern. Never respond to what you wish they had said — respond to what they actually said. Move the conversation offline as fast as possible.

**The strategic note for Kai:** A prospect reading a 1-star review is evaluating risk. They are not deciding that this gym is bad — they are deciding whether the gym handles problems like a business worth trusting. A humble, accountable owner response to a negative review is one of the highest-converting pieces of content on your entire Google Business Profile. Respond within 24 hours. Every time.

"[Reviewer name] — thank you for leaving this. I'm not going to offer an explanation right now because what matters is that we didn't deliver the experience you deserved, and I'd like to understand exactly what happened. I'm [Owner name], and I'd really appreciate the chance to talk with you directly. Please reach out to me at [owner email or phone] whenever you're ready. I'm listening."

---

### Template 5 — Fake or Spam Review
**When to use:** The review contains wrong location details, wrong business name, no membership history, generic language consistent with coordinated spam, or is clearly not from a member.
**Under 50 words.**
**Post-response action:** Flag for removal via Google Business Profile manager. Document the date, the review content, and the flagging action. If multiple suspicious reviews arrive within a short window, escalate to Kai immediately.

"Thank you for the feedback. We weren't able to find any record of a visit or membership associated with your account. If there's been a mix-up with a location, we're happy to help get you connected to the right place. You're also welcome to reach out directly at [owner email]."

---

### Template 6 — Specific Staff Callout (Positive)
**When to use:** Reviewer named a specific coach or staff member by name and credited them with their experience.
**Under 75 words.**
**Personalization note:** Share this response with the staff member named before posting it. Let them see it. This is retention and culture content, not just a customer service moment.

"[Reviewer name] — reading this out loud to [Staff name] right now. What you described — [specific detail they mentioned about the staff member] — that's exactly who [he/she/they] is every single day, and it means a lot to have you say it. Thank you for taking the time. This one gets saved."

---

### Template 7 — Long-Term Member Review
**When to use:** Reviewer mentions they've been a member for 6 months, a year, or more. Their review carries tenure credibility.
**Under 75 words.**
**Personalization note:** Acknowledge the tenure specifically. A long-term member reviewing voluntarily is an unusual act of loyalty — name it.

"[Reviewer name] — [X months / a year / however long they mentioned]. Thank you for being here that long, and thank you for writing this. Reviews from members who've been through the full experience mean something different than a review from the first week. Prospects reading this will know what they're getting — and that's because of people like you."

---

### Template 8 — Review Mentions Overcoming Initial Fear or Prior Gym Failure
**When to use:** The reviewer specifically references being intimidated before joining, having quit gyms before, or being surprised that it worked this time. This is the lifestyle member's story told publicly.
**Under 75 words.**
**Personalization note:** This review is the most valuable acquisition asset on your profile. The response should honor the vulnerability it took to write it publicly.

"[Reviewer name] — what you wrote about [the fear / the previous attempts / the surprise that it stuck] is the most honest version of what we're actually trying to build here. Someone is going to read this and finally decide to come in. Thank you for writing the true version. It matters more than you know."
