# Ad Copy — The No-Risk Comeback — Variant A

---

## AD SET 1: COLD AUDIENCE

**AUDIENCE TEMPERATURE:** Cold
**SCHWARTZ LEVEL:** Level 3 — Solution Aware

**HOOK:**
You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped showing up.

**PRIMARY TEXT:**
You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped showing up.

Most gyms hand you a key card and wish you luck. You go for three weeks. Life gets busy. Nobody calls. Nobody notices. And somehow that feels like your fault.

It isn't.

Staying consistent is almost impossible when you're doing it entirely alone. That's not a character flaw — it's just how habits actually work. They need structure. They need someone paying attention.

There's a different way to do this. One where someone actually checks in. Where you don't walk in on day one and figure it out by yourself. Where quitting is harder because someone would notice.

If you've been through the start-and-stop cycle more times than you'd like to count — this is worth reading.

**HEADLINE:** It wasn't you. It was the system.

**DESCRIPTION:** Most gyms leave you to figure it out alone. Here's what happens when they don't.

**CTA BUTTON:** Learn More

**CREATIVE DIRECTION:**
Shot type: Photo (static) or 15–20 second video with text overlay only, no voiceover
Subject: One person, 35–50 years old, not a fitness model — real proportions, real face, real tiredness and warmth coexisting
Setting: A park bench or a back porch in late afternoon light. The person is watching something off-camera — a kid playing, a dog running, nothing shown. Just their gaze. Comfortable clothes. A coffee cup or water bottle nearby that's gone cold.
Mood/lighting: Golden hour or overcast natural light. Nothing bright or energetic. The mood is quiet and a little wistful — not sad, just honest. This is a person who is living their life and quietly aware something is missing.
Text overlay (if video): Hook text appears at the 3-second mark in clean white serif type over a slightly darkened lower third. No animation. No flashy transitions. Just the words.
What the image must NOT show: Gym equipment, workout clothes, fitness imagery, anyone with a visible athletic physique, any before/after framing, any body part isolation (stomach, arms, thighs). Nothing that looks like a gym ad.

**COMPLIANCE CHECK:**
Meta Fitness Policy:
- [x] No specific weight loss claims
- [x] No before/after imagery described
- [x] No body-shaming or negative body idealization language
- [x] No countdown timers specified that are not genuine
- [x] No health condition targeting described
- [x] Landing page delivers exactly what this ad promises
Result: PASS

**TARGETING BRIEF:**
Audiences: Facebook interest targeting — Health & Wellness (general), Self-improvement, Work-life balance, Parenting (kids ages 5–16), Running (lapsed interest), Healthy lifestyle. Behaviors: Engaged shoppers, people who have interacted with fitness content but show no recent gym engagement. Age 32–54.
Placement: Facebook Feed primary. Instagram Feed secondary. Do not run in Stories — the creative requires dwell time.
Exclusions: Current page followers (move to warm), anyone who has visited the Anytime Fitness website in the last 180 days (move to retargeting), current members (suppress via CRM list upload)
Estimated audience size: Large — broad interest targeting, top of funnel

## AD SET 2: WARM AUDIENCE

**AUDIENCE TEMPERATURE:** Warm
**SCHWARTZ LEVEL:** Level 3 — Solution Aware

**HOOK:**
You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped showing up.

**PRIMARY TEXT:**
You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped showing up.

You've been here before — following along, thinking about it, knowing you should do something. That's not nothing. That's actually the hardest part for most people.

Here's what's different about Anytime Fitness.

Every new member gets a real coach contact for their first 30 days. Not an app. Not a chatbot. A person who texts you twice a week and actually notices if you go quiet. Because we know exactly when people stop coming — and it's almost never about motivation. It's about nobody noticing.

Members who've been through our 30-day coached start tell us the same thing: "I've never actually stuck with a gym before."

If you've been thinking about this, the next step is simple. Come see it.

**HEADLINE:** Someone actually notices here.

**DESCRIPTION:** Real coach contact. Twice a week. Your first 30 days aren't something you figure out alone.

**CTA BUTTON:** Learn More / Sign Up (A/B test button label separately)

**CREATIVE DIRECTION:**
Shot type: Photo (static) — one image, nothing staged
Subject: A coach and a single member, mid-conversation. The coach is gesturing toward something — a whiteboard, a piece of equipment, something with purpose. The member is listening and slightly leaning in. Both are real people. The coach does not look like a fitness model. Neither does the member. They look like two people who trust each other.
Setting: Inside Anytime Fitness — natural light from the front windows if available, or clean overhead gym lighting. The equipment is visible in the background but is not the subject. The relationship between the two people is the subject.
Mood/lighting: Warm and genuine. The mood is: competent, caring, not intimidating. This is what good coaching looks like — it's quiet and attentive, not loud and performative.
Text overlay: Optional. If used: "Your coach checks in. Every week." — clean white text in lower third. Understated.
What the image must NOT show: Groups of fit people working out intensely. Anyone posed. Anyone's body being the focal point. Any gym model aesthetic. Before/after visual cues of any kind. Stock photo feel.

- [x] No body-shaming or negative body idealization language

## AD SET 3: RETARGETING AUDIENCE

**AUDIENCE TEMPERATURE:** Retargeting
**SCHWARTZ LEVEL:** Level 5 — Most Aware

**HOOK:**
You're not lazy. You've just never had anyone notice when you stopped showing up. We notice.

**PRIMARY TEXT:**
You've seen this. You've thought about it. Here's everything you need to decide.

The No-Risk Comeback. $1 for your first 30 days at Anytime Fitness.

What that $1 gets you: a personal coach contact who checks in twice a week, a private orientation so you never walk in and figure it out alone, a done-for-you plan built around your schedule, and 24/7 access so you go when your life allows — not when a class schedule permits.

If you don't feel noticeably better in 30 days, you get your dollar back.

This isn't a gym membership offer. It's a structured way back in — for people who know what they need and have just never had the right support around it.

The next cohort starts soon. Spots are limited by design — each coach can only check in personally with so many people. If you're thinking about it, this week is the right time.

**HEADLINE:** $1. 30 days. A coach who notices.

**DESCRIPTION:** Your first month fully coached. If it doesn't work, you get your dollar back. No fine print.

**CTA BUTTON:** Get Offer

**CREATIVE DIRECTION:**
Shot type: Option A — Clean graphic with real photo background. Option B — 15-second talking-head video. Produce both and test.

Option A (graphic):
Background: A real photo of the Anytime Fitness interior, desaturated to 60% — present but not distracting. Clean and honest, not flashy.
Foreground text, centered:
Line 1 (large): $1
Line 2 (medium): Your first 30 days. Fully coached.
Line 3 (small, below a thin rule): Twice-weekly coach check-ins. Private orientation. 24/7 access. Money-back guarantee.
Line 4 (small): Anytime Fitness — [Location]
Color treatment: White text on the desaturated photo. No countdown timers. No urgency graphics. Just clean, clear, honest information.

Option B (talking-head video):
Subject: The gym owner or head coach — whoever is more natural on camera. Not scripted-feeling. Direct eye contact. Real clothes (not a polo with the gym logo unless that's what they naturally wear).
Setting: Inside the gym, but not in front of equipment — near the front desk or a quiet corner. This is a conversation, not a commercial.
Script (spoken naturally, not read): "You've been looking at this. I know — I can see when people come back to the page. Here's the thing. What we're offering is $1 for your first 30 days, and a coach who actually checks in on you twice a week. Not a bot. Me, or one of my coaches. We know when the first 30 days is the hardest part — because nobody's ever gotten past it with you before. If it doesn't work, you get your dollar back. The next group starts [day]. If you've been thinking about it — this is me telling you: come in."
Duration: 15 seconds maximum. No music. No B-roll. Just the coach talking.
What the image must NOT show: Countdown timers of any kind. Before/after imagery. Body-focused shots. Anything that looks like a pressure tactic.

**COMPLIANCE CHECK:**
Meta Fitness Policy:
- [x] No specific weight loss claims
- [x] No before/after imagery described
- [x] No body-shaming or negative body idealization language
- [x] No countdown timers specified that are not genuine
- [x] No health condition targeting described
- [x] Landing page delivers exactly what this ad promises
Result: PASS

**Note on scarcity language:** "Spots are limited by design" and "the next cohort starts soon" are both used in the primary text. This language is compliant only if the cohort structure and coach capacity limit are genuine. Before this ad goes live, Kai must confirm: (1) the cohort model is real and operational, (2) the coach-to-member ratio creates a genuine cap, and (3) the start date is set. If any of these are not yet confirmed, remove the scarcity language and replace with: "The next cohort starts the first Monday of the month." Do not run scarcity claims without Kai's verification that they are accurate.

**TARGETING BRIEF:**
Audiences: Website visitors (last 30 days), lead form openers who did not submit (last 30 days), video viewers 75%+ of any prior video content, anyone who clicked a prior ad but did not convert
Placement: Facebook Feed, Instagram Feed, Instagram Stories. Stories creative may need to be adapted to vertical format — the talking-head video (Option B) adapts naturally; the graphic (Option A) will need a vertical version produced.
Exclusions: Anyone who has already submitted a lead form (move to GHL nurture — Jessica handles from here), current members (suppress via CRM list upload)
Estimated audience size: Small — this is a high-intent, narrow audience. Expect smaller reach, significantly higher conversion rate than cold.
