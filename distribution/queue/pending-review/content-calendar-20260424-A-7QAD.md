# SECTION 1: CORNERSTONE CONCEPT BRIEF — VARIANT A

```
CORNERSTONE CONCEPT — VARIANT A

Real moment to document:
A member (ideally 40–50, parent, visibly not a fitness model) on a spring hike, bike ride,
or outdoor walk with their kids or partner. The camera captures them NOT stopping. Not
breathing hard. Keeping up. The moment after — in the car, at the trailhead, on the
porch — where they say something simple: "I couldn't have done that six months ago."
That's the entire cornerstone. That one sentence. Everything else is derived from it.

If this specific member and moment don't yet exist on film: the copy is written as a
composite with full transparency. "A member of ours — a 44-year-old parent — told us
something this week." Named when consent exists. Composite when it doesn't.

Why this is the right cornerstone this month:
- Active offer: The No-Risk Comeback speaks directly to the person who has quit before.
  The cornerstone is the proof that the comeback actually works.
- Winning hook 1: "The moment you couldn't keep up with your own kids." The cornerstone
  is the inverse of that hook — the resolution. Same emotional frequency, opposite
  direction. Hook creates the wound; cornerstone shows it healed.
- Seasonal truth: Late April. Spring is here. People are going outside. This is not a
  gym-in-January story. This is a right-now story happening to people they know.
  The avatar reads this and thinks: that could be happening for me by July.

The emotional core:
A person just like them — who quit before, who had the same doubts — is outside right
now doing the thing they haven't been able to do, and they feel nothing except good.

Where to capture first:
Instagram Reel (60 seconds) — then repurpose to Facebook video, then strip audio for
voiceover content, then pull quotes for static posts. Everything starts with the member
talking. Nothing starts with the gym talking about itself.

Production requirement:
Phone camera only. Natural light. No ring light, no lavalier mic, no B-roll cutaways
unless they happen naturally. The rawness is the point. If it looks produced, it loses
the thing that makes it work.
```
