## Lifetime Value (LTV)

**Standard member LTV:** ~$975 (18-month average retention × $54.17/month)
**PT member LTV:** Often exceeds $1,500–$2,500 depending on package and retention
**Acquisition cost target:** Keep CAC below $100 for standard membership (10:1+ LTV:CAC)

---

## Differentiators — What Makes This Location Win

**1. 24/7 access with global key fob**
No other local gym matches this. The 5am person and the 11pm person both have a home here. Travel anywhere, train everywhere.

**2. Coaching culture, not anonymous floor**
Staff know member names. Coaches do onboarding sessions. Someone notices when a member goes dark for two weeks and reaches out. This is the structural anti-churn mechanism.

**3. Size: small enough to matter, equipped enough to serve**
Not a boutique where you're paying $200/month for one class format. Not a big box where you're a number. Cardio, free weights, machines, functional training area — everything a committed adult needs.

**4. GymSuite AI integration (Jessica)**
Every lead enters an automated nurture sequence. Every member gets automated accountability touchpoints. Birthday messages. Milestone recognitions. The retention infrastructure runs whether staff are in the building or not.

**5. The $1 trial offer removes all financial risk**
The single biggest barrier to trying a new gym is fear of paying and quitting. The $1/30-day trial makes the first step cost nothing meaningful. The small payment creates commitment (Cialdini: Consistency).

## Competitive Context

**Primary competitor type:** Big box gyms (Planet Fitness, LA Fitness) — compete on price
**Secondary competitor type:** Home workout apps (Peloton, Apple Fitness+, YouTube) — compete on convenience
**Tertiary competitor type:** Other boutique/class gyms (OrangeTheory, CrossFit) — compete on brand/intensity

**Our positioning vs. each:**
- vs. Big box: "They know your credit card number, not your name. We know both."
- vs. Home apps: "You can work out at home. You can't get accountability at home."
- vs. Boutique: "Not $200/month for one format. Everything you need, at a price you'll keep paying for years."
- vs. Doing nothing: "The decision to not decide is still a decision."

## What AHRI Should Always Keep in Mind

1. **Retention is as important as acquisition.** One month more retention per member at 300 members = $16,251 additional annual revenue. Every accountability asset has financial value.

2. **PT upsell is the profit lever.** When content or offers can naturally introduce personal training as a next step for members who are succeeding, flag it to Kai.

3. **The $1 trial is a proven offer.** Do not reinvent this. The small payment creates commitment and removes financial objection simultaneously. It is used as the entry point for all cohort-based acquisition campaigns.

4. **GHL is live.** Jessica is already nurturing leads. AHRI's job is to get leads INTO GHL with enough pre-qualification that Jessica's nurture converts efficiently.

5. **Never run budget without Kai's approval.** Creative approved ≠ budget approved. Always separate. Always explicit. Always with exact numbers.
